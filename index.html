Creative engineering in the tradition of:

Ada Lovelace
(the first computer programmer)

&

Leonardo da Vinci
(artist and inventor)

___________________________

Ada & Leo is a new kids' tech program focused on learning and creativity, led by veteran programmer and teacher Ben Wheeler.

The program is open to students age 6-9.

The cost is $300 and covers materials.

Topics include programming, circuitry, 3d printing and design, robotics, strategic thinking, invention and design thinking. We will use creative and effective software and hardware tools and will play games that stimulate logic thinking.

Location: 483 Putnam Ave, between Throop and Tompkins Aves in Bed-Stuy, Brooklyn.

Topics:

Programming games and other interactive programs
using several of the following languages and platforms:
• Scratch, from MIT
• Bitsbox
• Begin to Code (Ben’s in-progress “choose your own adventure” platform)
• Pencilcode
• Snap
concepts:
• designing ideas for a program
• following tutorial instructions
• experimenting with using code to solve problems
• stepping through code to recreate computer logic
• debugging

Circuitry
using:
• batteries
• LED lights
• play-doh
• paper
• switches
• Makey Makey
concepts:
• circuits as a closed, continuous loop
• conductivity
• investigating circuits that aren’t working
• how soldering works

Robotics
using:
• Lego Mindstorms
• batteries
• motors
• wires
concepts:
• following step-by-step instructions
• dividing up tasks and negotiating roles
• deciding which project to take on as a group

3d design
using:
• TinkerCAD
• Printrbot Simple Metal 3d printer
concepts:
• 3d drawing and imagining
• creating and manipulating 3d shapes
• how 3d printers print
• how machines make mistakes
• fixing the things that go wrong with delicate procedures

All teachers are trained in first aid and have passed background checks. The program is safety-conscious.

Even more importantly, we promise to care for the heart, mind and spirit of curiosity in your child as if they were our own children... (who will be in the class too!)

Who we are

Ben Wheeler

Ben is a veteran developer and teacher in Brooklyn. He’s taught hundreds of people to program, helped foment a revolution, and makes a mean gumbo. His work has been published in the Harvard International Review and The Best of Make Magazine. You can see even more of his eclectic interests at techno-social.com.

Audrey Fox

Audrey is a MFA candidate in Design and Technology at Parsons in New York City. She is an experienced branding designer and enjoys stints working as well as a tech educator for children. Her current work focuses on using sensors, computer vision and Internet of Things to build multi-sensory interactive environments. See more at artificialforest.com.

Ayodamola Okunseinde

Ayodamola ("Ayo" for short) is an artist and interactive designer, and is currently an adjunct faculty member at The New School, Parsons School of Design. His works range from painting and speculative design to physically interactive works and explorations of Afrofuturism. See more of his work at ayo.io.
